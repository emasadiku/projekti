4-Day Vienna Itinerary

Dates for February: 6, 14, 21

Price: 300€

Day 1: Imperial Vienna

Morning: Schönbrunn Palace

Start your trip by visiting Schönbrunn Palace, the former summer residence of the Habsburg family. Explore its beautiful gardens and opulent interiors. Don’t forget to check out the Gloriette for panoramic views of the city.

Afternoon: Belvedere Palace

In the afternoon, head to Belvedere Palace, a masterpiece of Baroque architecture. The Upper Belvedere houses a stunning collection of Austrian art, including Gustav Klimt’s famous "The Kiss." Enjoy the serene gardens surrounding the palace as well.

Evening: Dinner at a Traditional Viennese Restaurant

End your day with a traditional Viennese dinner at one of the city's renowned restaurants. Try Wiener Schnitzel or Tafelspitz, accompanied by a glass of Austrian wine.

Day 2: City Center and Historic Sites

Morning: St. Stephen’s Cathedral

Begin your day with a visit to St. Stephen’s Cathedral, a Gothic masterpiece in the heart of Vienna. Climb the tower for breathtaking views of the city, or explore the catacombs beneath the church.

Afternoon: Hofburg Palace and the Spanish Riding School

Next, head to the Hofburg Palace, the former imperial residence of the Habsburg dynasty. Visit the Hofburg's numerous attractions, including the Imperial Apartments, the Sisi Museum, and the Spanish Riding School, home to the famous Lipizzaner horses.

Evening: Vienna State Opera

Experience the magic of Vienna’s classical music scene by attending a performance at the Vienna State Opera. Whether you enjoy a ballet or an opera, it’s an unforgettable experience in one of the world's finest opera houses.

Day 3: Art, Culture, and Coffee

Morning: Kunsthistorisches Museum

Spend your morning at the Kunsthistorisches Museum (Museum of Art History), which houses an impressive collection of artworks, including pieces by Raphael, Rembrandt, and Vermeer.

Afternoon: Prater Park and the Giant Ferris Wheel

In the afternoon, head to Prater Park, a large public park famous for the historic Giant Ferris Wheel. Enjoy a ride for spectacular views of Vienna, or simply take a walk through the park's expansive green spaces.

Evening: Café Central

Vienna is known for its café culture, and Café Central is one of the city's most iconic spots. Enjoy a coffee and a traditional Austrian pastry, such as Sachertorte, while soaking in the historical ambiance.

Day 4: Modern Vienna and Relaxation

Morning: Hundertwasserhaus

Start your day with a visit to Hundertwasserhaus, a colorful and unconventional apartment building designed by the Austrian artist Friedensreich Hundertwasser. The building’s whimsical design and unique colors make it a popular landmark in Vienna.

Afternoon: MuseumsQuartier

Next, explore the MuseumsQuartier, a cultural complex that houses some of Vienna's best contemporary art museums, including the Leopold Museum and the MUMOK. Take some time to relax in the courtyards or enjoy a meal at one of the many trendy cafés.

Evening: Danube River Cruise

Finish your stay in Vienna with a relaxing Danube River cruise. Enjoy dinner on board while taking in the stunning views of the city’s landmarks illuminated at night.
