Explore Paris

Everything you need to know before visiting

Essential Info
  • Currency: Euro (€)
  • Language: French (learn basic phrases like "Bonjour" (Hello), "Merci" (Thank you), and "S'il vous plaît" (Please))
  • Getting Around:
    • -> The metro is the fastest and most convenient way to travel.
    • -> Walking is ideal for exploring neighborhoods.
    • -> Bikes and electric scooters are widely available for rent (e.g., Vélib’).
    • -> Taxis and ride-hailing apps like Uber are available but can be pricey.
Neighborhoods to Explore
  • Le Marais: Trendy area full of boutiques, galleries, and cafes. A great place for art lovers.
  • Montmartre: Famous for the Sacré-Cœur Basilica and stunning views of Paris.
  • Latin Quarter: A historic and vibrant student district with great food and bookshops.
  • Champs-Élysées: Famous avenue with luxury shops, theaters, and cafes.
  • Le Quartier Pigalle: Known for its nightlife and historic cabarets like the Moulin Rouge.
Famous Landmarks
  • Eiffel Tower: A must-visit symbol of Paris, with breathtaking views from the top.
  • Louvre Museum: Home to world-famous artworks like the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo.
  • Notre-Dame Cathedral: A Gothic masterpiece that has stood for centuries.
  • Arc de Triomphe: A monument to France’s military victories, with panoramic views of the city.
  • Sainte-Chapelle: A stunning gothic chapel known for its incredible stained glass windows.
Best Restaurants

For French Cuisine:

  • Le Procope: The oldest café in Paris, serving classic French dishes.
  • Bistrot Paul Bert: Known for its steak-frites.
  • Septime: A Michelin-starred modern French restaurant.

Pastry and Desserts:

  • Ladurée: Famous for its macarons.
  • Pierre Hermé: Unique and innovative pastries.

Coffee Spots:

  • Café de Flore: An iconic Parisian café.
  • Coutume Café: Known for its specialty coffee.

Wine Bars:

  • Le Baron Rouge: A traditional wine bar.
  • Frenchie Bar à Vins: A modern take on wine and small plates.
Best Time to Visit
  • Spring (April to June): Mild weather and blooming gardens.
  • Fall (September to November): Pleasant weather with fewer crowds.
  • Winter (December to February): Perfect for cozying up in cafes and experiencing Christmas markets.
  • Avoid: August, as many locals are on holiday, and some businesses close.
Shopping in Paris
  • Champs-Élysées: Iconic avenue with luxury brands like Louis Vuitton and Chanel.
  • Le Marais: Trendy district filled with independent boutiques and vintage stores.
  • Rue Saint-Honoré: Famous for high-fashion shops and Parisian designers.
  • Rue de Rivoli: A bustling street offering everything from souvenir shops to renowned fashion brands.
  • Boulevard Haussmann: Home to the grand department stores like Galeries Lafayette.
Tips for Tourists
  • Plan in Advance: Book tickets for popular attractions like the Eiffel Tower and Louvre Museum online to avoid long lines.
  • Understand Meal Times: Lunch is typically from 12 PM–2 PM, and dinner starts around 7 PM.
  • Be Polite: Always greet shopkeepers and waitstaff with "Bonjour" or "Bonsoir" before speaking.
  • Avoid Tourist Traps: Skip restaurants near major attractions with "menu touristique" signs. Head to side streets for authentic dining experiences.
  • Use Public Transport Passes: The Paris Visite Pass or Navigo Easy card can save money on transport.
  • Stay Aware of Pickpockets: Especially in crowded areas like the metro and tourist spots.
  • Walk as Much as Possible: Paris is best experienced on foot, with its hidden streets and charming neighborhoods.
Bonus Experiences
  • Day Trips:
    • Versailles Palace: Explore the luxurious palace and its gardens.
    • Giverny: Visit Monet’s house and gardens, a haven for art lovers.
    • Champagne Region: Perfect for wine enthusiasts.
  • Hidden Gems:
    • Canal Saint-Martin: A trendy area with great cafes and shops.
    • Passage des Panoramas: One of Paris's oldest covered passages, filled with boutiques.
  • Food Tours: Discover Paris’s culinary delights with a guided tour through markets and bakeries.
  • Paris by Night: Enjoy the Eiffel Tower's sparkling lights and nighttime views of the Seine.
