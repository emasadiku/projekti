Explore Rome

Everything you need to know before visiting

Essential Info
  • Currency: Euro (€)
  • Language: Italian (basic phrases like "Ciao" (Hello), "Grazie" (Thank you), "Per favore" (Please) are helpful)
  • Getting Around:
    • -> Metro, buses, and trams are convenient for getting around.
    • -> Walking is the best way to explore the historic center.
    • -> Taxis are expensive; apps like Free Now or ItTaxi are better for booking.
Top Attractions
  • Colosseum & Roman Forum: The iconic amphitheater and nearby ruins of ancient Rome.
  • Vatican City: Home to St. Peter’s Basilica, the Sistine Chapel, and the Vatican Museums.
  • Pantheon: A remarkably preserved Roman temple with an open dome.
  • Trevi Fountain: Toss a coin to ensure a return trip to Rome.
  • Piazza Navona: A beautiful square featuring fountains and street performers.
Best Restaurants

For Traditional Roman Cuisine:

  • Roscioli: Known for its carbonara and fine Italian deli.
  • Felice a Testaccio: Famous for cacio e pepe.
  • Trattoria Pennestri: A modern twist on classic dishes.

Pizza Spots:

  • Pizzarium Bonci: Known for its gourmet pizza al taglio (by the slice).
  • Emma Pizzeria con Cucina: Thin-crust Roman-style pizza.

Gelato Shops:

  • Gelateria del Teatro: Unique flavors like sage and raspberry.
  • Giolitti: One of the oldest and most famous gelaterias.
Best Time to Visit
  • Spring (April to June): Ideal weather, blooming flowers, and lively piazzas.
  • Fall (September to November): Mild weather, fewer crowds, and harvest festivals.
  • Avoid: Summer (July-August) due to heat and crowds; many locals take vacations during this time.
Shopping in Rome
  • Via del Corso: A major shopping street with international brands and Italian designers.
  • Via Condotti: The luxury shopping street near the Spanish Steps.
  • Campo de' Fiori: A bustling market where you can buy local goods and souvenirs.
Tips for Tourists
  • Plan in Advance: Book tickets for major attractions like the Colosseum and Vatican Museums online to skip long lines.
  • Dress Modestly for Churches: Shoulders and knees must be covered.
  • Carry Cash: Many smaller shops and restaurants don’t accept credit cards.
  • Avoid Tourist Traps:
    • Avoid dining at places with multilingual menus or pushy waiters.
    • Restaurants in tourist hotspots are usually overpriced.
  • Be Aware of Pickpockets: Especially around crowded attractions and public transportation.
  • Tipping is Optional: It’s appreciated but not expected, as service is often included in the bill.
  • Tap Water is Drinkable: Look for free public fountains called "nasoni."
  • Use Public Transport Passes: Get a Roma Pass for unlimited rides and discounted attraction entries.
Bonus Experiences
  • Day Trips:
    • Tivoli: Visit Villa d’Este and Villa Adriana.
    • Castelli Romani: Famous for its wine and countryside views.
  • Food Tours: Join a guided tour to discover the best local cuisine.
  • Evening Walks: Stroll around illuminated landmarks like the Colosseum and Trevi Fountain for a magical experience.
