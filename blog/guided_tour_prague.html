4-Day Prague Itinerary

Dates for February: 7, 14, 21

Price: 200€

Day 1: Old Town and Historic Landmarks

Morning: Old Town Square and Astronomical Clock

Start your day by exploring the heart of Prague, the Old Town Square. Marvel at the iconic Astronomical Clock and watch the clock's hourly show. Take a stroll through the narrow streets of the Old Town and discover its medieval charm.

Afternoon: Charles Bridge and Vltava River

Cross the famous Charles Bridge, a historical landmark that connects the Old Town with the Lesser Town. Along the way, admire the views of the Vltava River and the city. Stop to appreciate the statues and enjoy a scenic walk along the bridge.

Evening: Dinner at a Traditional Czech Restaurant

End your day by enjoying a delicious meal at one of Prague's traditional Czech restaurants. Try local specialties like goulash, svíčková, or a hearty Czech beer.

Day 2: Prague Castle and Gardens

Morning: Prague Castle

Begin the day with a visit to Prague Castle, one of the largest ancient castles in the world. Explore the castle's various courtyards, gardens, and palaces, including the stunning St. Vitus Cathedral, the official seat of the Archbishop of Prague.

Afternoon: Golden Lane and Castle Gardens

Take a walk down Golden Lane, a picturesque street lined with colorful houses that once housed castle guards and craftsmen. Then, wander through the serene castle gardens for a relaxing afternoon in the heart of Prague.

Evening: Petrin Hill and Observation Tower

In the evening, head to Petrin Hill for stunning views of the city. Take a funicular ride to the top and visit the Petrin Observation Tower, which offers a fantastic panoramic view of Prague's skyline.

Day 3: Jewish Quarter and Museums

Morning: Jewish Quarter (Josefov)

Explore the historic Jewish Quarter of Prague, Josefov. Visit the Old Jewish Cemetery and the Spanish Synagogue, which houses exhibits on Jewish history and culture in Prague.

Afternoon: National Museum and Wenceslas Square

Spend the afternoon at the National Museum, one of the most important museums in the country, featuring exhibits on natural history, culture, and art. Afterward, take a stroll down Wenceslas Square, a hub of historical events and shopping.

Evening: Dinner Cruise on the Vltava River

Finish the day with a scenic dinner cruise on the Vltava River. Enjoy a delicious meal while taking in the beautiful sights of Prague illuminated at night, including the Prague Castle and Charles Bridge.

Day 4: Modern Prague and Relaxation

Morning: Vysehrad Fortress and Park

Start your day with a visit to Vysehrad, a historic fort overlooking the Vltava River. Explore the grounds, take in the views of Prague, and relax in the surrounding park. Don’t miss the beautiful Vysehrad Cemetery, where notable figures of Czech history are buried.

Afternoon: Dancing House and Vltava Riverwalk

Head to the Dancing House, one of Prague’s most iconic modern buildings. Afterward, take a leisurely walk along the Vltava River, soaking in the beautiful views and the charming cityscape.

Evening: Explore Prague’s Beer Culture

End your visit by discovering Prague’s renowned beer culture. Visit one of the many local pubs to try traditional Czech beers and learn about the country's centuries-old brewing tradition.
